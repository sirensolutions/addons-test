# AML Investigation Demo  
### Entity Resolution + Graph Intelligence + AI

## Overview

This demo showcases how financial crime and compliance teams can uncover hidden risks across fragmented datasets.

Organizations often need to investigate potential money laundering, fraud, tax exposure, or compliance risks linked to registered companies and individuals.

However, data typically exists in silos — spread across corporate registries, transaction systems, tax systems, and trade databases — making it difficult to detect hidden connections.

This demo demonstrates how Entity Resolution, graph visualization, and AI-driven analytics transform disconnected data into actionable investigative insight.

---

## The Business Challenge

Investigators face several challenges:

- Duplicate or inconsistent entity records
- Slight variations in names or addresses
- Fragmented systems with no unified view
- Hidden connections between people and companies
- High volumes of transactions with limited risk visibility
- Time-consuming manual reconciliation

For example:
- “Delta Solutions” vs “Delta Solutions Pte Ltd”
- “Suresh Nair” vs “S. Nair”

Without intelligent matching, these may appear as separate entities — masking real risk exposure.

---

## Data Integrated in the Demo

The demo combines multiple structured datasets such as:

- Company registration records
- Directors and shareholders
- Company–person relationships
- Tax reporting data
- Financial transactions (including high-risk flags)
- Cross-border trade activity

By integrating these sources, we create a connected data ecosystem ready for analysis.

---

## How the Demo Works

### 1️⃣ Entity Resolution (ER)

The system automatically detects and merges duplicate or similar entities.

**Person Resolution**
- Identifies variations of the same individual
- Merges them into a single unified profile

**Company Resolution**
- Matches similar company names
- Consolidates related records across systems

This removes duplication and reveals the true structure of relationships.

---

### 2️⃣ Relationship Visualization

Once entities are resolved, the graph view shows:

- Who controls which companies
- Shared directors across multiple businesses
- Links between companies and transactions
- Cross-border trade activity connected to entities

This provides investigators with a complete, connected picture.

---

### 3️⃣ High-Risk Transaction Analysis

The demo allows filtering by transaction risk level.

By combining risk flags with resolved entities and graph analytics, the system:

- Highlights concentrated risk exposure
- Detects hidden transaction chains
- Identifies indirect relationships
- Surfaces suspicious activity clusters

Seemingly unrelated entities may become visibly connected once duplicates are resolved.
